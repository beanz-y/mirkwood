# MIRKWOOD (Myrkviðr)

*A cooperative game of paths and dwindling light for four souls. Inspired by
The Night Cage (Smirk & Dagger); all rules text here is Mirkwood's own, and
every mechanical departure is catalogued in the numbered list at the end.*

---

## Welcome to Myrkviðr

*No skald will sing of how you died. No shield-wall broke around you, no
valkyrie stooped out of the storm to carry you up to the Allfather's benches.
Yours was a straw-death — quiet, unwitnessed, unchosen — and what met you on
the far side of it was not the mead-bright roar of a hall. It was wind moving
through branches.*

*You stand beneath the black boughs of **Myrkviðr**, the dark wood that has
marked the border between the worlds since the first days. Swan-maids have
flown through it; gods and heroes have ridden its edges; the sons of Muspell
will cross it burning when the world ends. Nothing in it keeps its shape. The
mist of Niflheim breathes between the trunks, and wherever it settles, the
forest forgets its own paths — a road walked once is not there when you turn
around.*

*Yet the dead here whisper of two doors. Somewhere under the boughs stands
**Valgrind**, the corpse-gate of Valhalla, behind which the einherjar feast
and fight until Ragnarök calls them out. And somewhere stands the gate of
**Fólkvangr**, Freyja's field, where her hall Sessrúmnir rises and she seats
those dead she chooses as her own. Odin did not choose you; Freyja did not
call your name. So you will make the gates take you: find the standing stones
where the old rune-rings wait, cut their marks into your soul, and come before
a gate that cannot refuse four souls bearing its four runes.*

*You carry no candle or torch — such fires died with your body. What you carry
is an **Ember of Hope**, and it is enough: where your hope shines, the paths
hold. Where it moves on, the mist devours what it leaves behind. And you are
not alone beneath the boughs. The **draugar** walk here — the restless dead
who sought the gates before you and failed, and rose again with nothing left
in them but spite for every soul that still dares to hope.*

Mirkwood (Myrkviðr) is a cooperative game of paths and dwindling light: four
souls, played by one to four players, who escape together or not at all. Each
turn your hope kindles fresh paths at the edge of the light, while the mist
devours everything left unlit — the forest is never the same shape twice. The
shared path stack is the party's hope made visible; every tile drawn, burned,
or lost to the dark brings Niflheim's Embrace a step closer. Gather four
different runes of one gate, bring all four souls to stand on it together,
and walk out of Myrkviðr — or let the mist take everything.

## Object

The four souls **win together** if all four stand on the *same* Gate, each
bearing a **different rune** of that gate's set (all four Valhalla runes, or
all four Fólkvangr runes, spread across the four souls).

The souls **lose together** if:

- Both Gates are swallowed by the mist before being placed.
- Too few Rune Circles remain for the souls to complete the four marks a gate
  demands (checked automatically: each soul not yet holding a distinct rune of
  the best available gate needs one remaining circle — and a circle a soul is
  already standing on is spent, for its stones speak to each soul only once).
- A falling soul has no tile left to land on (they fall through the void
  forever). With the stack spent this fate is sealed — and announced — the
  moment they fall.
- During **Niflheim's Embrace**, no gate remains that all four souls can still
  reach bearing a completable set of runes (checked automatically after every
  turn: once the stack is dead the path network only shrinks, so a severed
  road stays severed).
- The group concedes to Niflheim.

## Components (digital)

- **6×6 board.** The edges wrap: the forest loops back on itself, as Myrkviðr
  famously turns travelers in circles.
- **4 Start tiles** (one per soul; 2 pathways; *Fractured*).
- **Path stack of 74 tiles** (the group's shared reserve of hope):
  - 10 Straight paths (*Fractured*)
  - 32 Forking paths (2 of them *Fractured*)
  - 12 Crossroads
  - 6 Rune Circles (crossroads; *Fractured*)
  - 12 Draugar (crossroads)
  - 2 Gates — one to Valhalla, one to Fólkvangr (**one doorway only**)
- The first 8 tiles of the stack are always plain paths (2 straight, 4 forking,
  2 crossroads), so the first lights kindled never wake anything.

The stack is the timer. Every tile drawn, discarded, or lost to the mist is
hope burned. **When the stack is empty, Niflheim's Embrace begins** (see below).

## Setup

In seat order, each soul:

1. Chooses any empty space and places their Start tile in any orientation.
2. Kindles their first paths: a tile is drawn for each of the Start tile's
   open passages and placed so the ways join (any rotation that connects
   is legal).

All souls begin **Hopeful** with **1 Resolve**.

---

## A Soul's Turn

On your turn you either **Stay** or **Move**. A Hopeless soul may first
**Rekindle** (see Hope).

### Stay
1. Gain **1 Resolve** (maximum 2). *(Hopeless souls may not Stay unless they
   spend 1 Resolve — and gain none.)*
2. Hope gutters while you linger: the top tile of the stack is turned face up
   and cast away.
   - **If it is a Draugr, it does not go quietly:** it claws onto the board,
     seizing the place of any one path tile joined to yours (your choice —
     never a Gate, never an occupied tile; if no path joins yours, it sinks
     back into the mist).
3. If you are standing on a **Fractured** tile, it crumbles beneath you —
   you fall into the Rift (your turn ends).

### Move
1. Step one space along a joined path. Two souls may never share a tile
   — **except Gates**, which hold any number.
2. If you left a Fractured tile, it crumbles into a **Rift** behind you.
3. Any Draugr that saw you move **strikes** (see The Draugar).
4. Tiles no longer lit by anyone's hope are lost to the mist, forever.
5. If you are Hopeful, **kindle the way**: at each open passage of your new
   tile, a tile is drawn from the stack and joined to the path — you choose
   the space and the rotation, one tile at a time.
6. You may spend Resolve to **press on** (up to two more steps a turn).

## Hope & Hopelessness

- **Hopeful:** the ember of your hope pierces one layer of mist — you kindle
  and hold lit all paths one space around you (no diagonals). A tile that
  walls off one of your passages stays visible (you can see the wall it makes).
- **Hopeless:** you see only the tile you stand on. The dark drives you
  onward — you **must Move every turn** (Staying costs 1 Resolve). You walk
  blind: stepping into empty mist, you draw a single tile for that space and
  turn it so it joins the way behind you. If it is a Draugr, it strikes at
  once and you claw your way off it.
- **Rekindling:** a Hopeless soul adjacent to a Hopeful soul along a joined
  pathway is rekindled automatically. A Hopeless soul may also spend
  **1 Resolve** at the start of their turn to rekindle their own hope
  *(a Mirkwood rule — it is what makes Rift falls survivable alone)*.

## Resolve

Souls harden their resolve in two ways (max 2 held):

- **Stand fast:** Stay on your turn (+1).
- **Defiance:** move through a Draugr's gaze and escape its strike
  untouched (+1).

Spend 1 Resolve to:

| Act | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Press On** | Take another Move after your move resolves (at most two extra steps a turn). |
| **Rekindle** | Regain Hope at the start of your turn. |
| **Endure** | Stay while Hopeless. |
| **Brace** | When a Draugr strikes you, 2 tiles burn instead of 3. |
| **Berserk** | Hurl yourself onto a Draugr in a fury: its strike always lands on you (Brace still allowed). Once its spite is spent, the Draugr is **banished from the forest** — its tile is lost — and you claw your way onto an adjacent space. |
| **Ward** | During Niflheim's Embrace, ward the forest: no tile is taken from the board at the end of your turn. |

## Fractured Paths & Void Rifts

Fractured tiles crumble into **Rifts** the moment you leave them (or at the
end of a turn you Stay on them). A Rift is a wound in the world — it blinds
the draugar, and a soul may deliberately leap into a connected Rift.

**Falling:** your turn ends and you plummet through the void. At the start of
your next turn, choose any empty, unlit space in the row **or** column of the
Rift you fell through, draw a tile to land on (any orientation), and land.

- **You land Hopeless** *(Mirkwood rule)* — spend 1 Resolve to Rekindle, or
  find a Hopeful friend.
- If you land on a Draugr, it strikes you and everything in its gaze; it then
  collapses into a Rift beneath you, and you claw your way onto an adjacent
  space.
- If there are no empty spaces in that row/column, land on an existing tile.
- **If the stack is empty and you must draw to land — you fall forever, and
  the game is lost.**

## The Draugar

The restless dead of Myrkviðr — souls that failed this road and rose again,
hateful of every light that still hopes. *(One draugr; many draugar.)*

- Draugar are drawn and placed like any tile (crossroads).
- A Draugr wakes at **motion in its corridors**: a soul stepping anywhere
  along a straight, unbroken run of path joined to it — however far away,
  even across the board's wrapped edges — rouses it, and it lashes down all
  four of its corridors at once.
- Rifts, empty spaces, and walls blind it.
- **Every soul caught in its gaze is struck:** 3 tiles burn from the stack
  per victim (2 with Brace) and that soul becomes **Hopeless**.
- Ending your move *outside* the corridor means the strike **misses you** —
  every other soul still in its gaze is struck as normal — and slipping its
  gaze steels your Resolve (+1).
- If a Draugr's gaze reaches another Draugr, that one too lashes out — a
  chain of spite.
- Falling drops you out of the world before the blow lands: a soul that falls
  rouses watching draugar but is missed by their strike.
- **Going Berserk** (1 Resolve) destroys a Draugr outright: its strike lands
  on you, you claw your way onto an adjacent space — and then its spite is
  spent, and the Draugr dissolves behind you, leaving bare ground. But it is
  not the only way past one: slip its gaze, or simply walk away — a Draugr no
  soul lights any longer fades back into the mist like any tile. (One *fallen
  onto* from the void collapses into a Rift; one merely stumbled onto in the
  dark remains — though the mist claims it before any rekindled hope can
  light it again.)

## Rune Circles

Ancient stone rings where the old marks can still be taken.

- Move onto a Rune Circle to **attune** to any one rune of either gate
  (a free action; you may also choose a rune already held by another soul,
  but duplicates can never open a gate).
- A soul bears **one rune at a time**; attuning again replaces the old mark.
  Runes are marks on the soul — **they cannot be passed between players**
  *(re-attunement is the safety valve instead; see departures #5)*.
- Rune Circles are Fractured: they crumble behind the soul who uses them.
- **Random Runes** (host variant, toggled in the lobby): the stones choose —
  instead of picking, you receive a random rune **not currently borne by any
  soul** (you may still decline and leave the circle untouched). Fate decides
  whether Valhalla or Fólkvangr calls you. To temper fate, a soul may
  **linger**: Staying on a Rune Circle does *not* crumble it in this variant —
  the turn passes and a tile burns from the stack as with any Stay, but the
  stones choose again, letting you fish for a mark that matches the party.
  The circle still crumbles into a Void Rift when you finally leave it.

## The Gates

- One Gate opens on **Valhalla** — the skalds name it **Valgrind**, the
  corpse-door that few know how to unlock — and one on **Fólkvangr**, Freyja's
  field, where her hall Sessrúmnir stands. Each Gate has a **single doorway**;
  it is placed like any tile (its doorway will face the passage it was
  revealed from).
- **Once placed, a Gate is permanent.** The mist cannot take it, and Niflheim's
  Embrace cannot remove it.
- A Gate still in the stack *can* be lost when tiles burn from the stack
  (draugr strikes, Staying). Watch the discard pile. If both gates are lost
  this way, the game is over.
- **Victory:** all four souls stand on the same Gate bearing four different
  runes of that gate's set.

## Niflheim's Embrace (endgame)

When the last tile leaves the stack, the primordial cold of Niflheim closes in:

- No new paths can ever be kindled.
- At the end of **every** soul's turn (after any extra steps), the group must
  surrender one remaining tile from the board (never a Gate, never a tile a
  soul stands on) — unless that soul spends 1 Resolve to **Ward** the forest.
- Play continues on the dwindling board until the souls win, are cut off,
  or fall.

## Design decisions & departures (flagged for review)

1. **Runes are true Elder Futhark, aligned to their gods** (table above). The
   draft's mislabeled glyphs were corrected in the first build; the mythos
   pass then moved Ansuz (Odin's rune) to Valhalla's set, gave Fólkvangr Fehu
   (the Vanir's wealth-rune, for Freyja), and retired Isa — ice is Niflheim's
   element, not a paradise's.
2. **Resolve from draugar:** paid on *evading* a triggered Draugr, not on being
   hit — being hit can't grant the Resolve that pays for Brace.
3. **Rekindle (new Resolve spend):** implements your "spend a Resolve to regain
   Hope after a fall," generalized to any start of turn. Without it, a soul
   who falls while alone could be unrecoverable.
4. **Gates:** exactly 2 (TNC: 4), single-doorway, permanent once placed, exempt
   from Niflheim removal (permanence would be meaningless otherwise). They can
   still be lost from the stack — that's the drama of the discard pile.
5. **No rune passing** (TNC allows passing keys). Re-attuning at circles is
   the replacement flexibility. If playtests feel too tight, add back a
   "transfer marks between adjacent souls" action.
6. **Fall landing:** you may choose the row *or* column of the rift at landing
   time (TNC forces you to commit when you fall). Slightly kinder; simpler online.
7. **Stay ends your turn** — you cannot Stay and then spend Resolve to keep
   moving (TNC's sequencing arguably allows it; disallowed here to keep Stay
   a real cost).
8. **Landing Hopeless** (your rule) is harsh but kept: with Rekindle + the
   auto-rekindle adjacency rule it plays fine.
9. **Tile mix:** kept TNC's ratios (2 of the forking paths are Fractured), and
   the stack opens with 8 plain paths as TNC's does, so the first reveals are
   never lethal.
10. **Clawing off a Draugr never re-triggers it** (it's part of the strike's
    resolution), and you may never deliberately claw your way onto another
    visible Draugr.
11. **Berserk banishes** (departure from TNC, whose Charge leaves the monster
    standing): the rushed Draugr disappears once its strike resolves and the
    berserker claws clear of it, making Berserk a true monster-removal tool —
    a Resolve plus a guaranteed hit buys a cleared corridor.
12. **Rekindled souls kindle their own paths** (departure from TNC, where the
    current player draws relit tiles) — playtest rule: a player always places
    their own tiles, even mid another player's turn.
13. **The mist sweeps before hope spreads**: after every arrival, unlit tiles
    are removed *before* the rekindle check — a monster (or path) abandoned in
    hopelessness cannot be saved by being rekindled a moment later.
14. **Terminology is Mirkwood's own** (2026-07-07 separation pass): Berserk /
    Ward / Press On replace the TNC-named spends Charge / Sustain / Move Again;
    *kindle* replaces TNC's Illuminate; the soul-light is an *Ember of Hope*
    (no candle-and-flicker vocabulary); *draugar* is used as the correct Old
    Norse plural in prose. All rule text was rewritten so no sentence tracks
    the TNC rulebook's phrasing.
15. **Mythos grounding** (same pass): Fólkvangr follows Grímnismál — Freyja
    *chooses* half the dead as her own (her hall is Sessrúmnir), rather than
    the modern "gate for the peaceful" reading; Valhalla's gate bears its
    attested name Valgrind (Grímnismál 22); the draugar are cast as restless
    dead — corporeal revenants of Norse folklore, not ghosts; default soul
    names are all Old Norse (Sigrun, a valkyrie's name, replaced Eira).
16. **Hopeless positions end themselves** (2026-07-08, from playtest): three
    auto-loss refinements so the saga never drags on after it is decided.
    (a) A Rune Circle a soul stands on counts as *spent* in the base game —
    its prompt never comes again, nobody else can enter, and it crumbles on
    leave — so "wrong rune at the last circle" is called immediately.
    (b) A soul falling with the stack spent is doomed the moment they fall
    (landing on an empty space needs a draw that can never come); the loss is
    declared at the fall, not a round later. This deliberately removes an
    obscure theoretical rescue — teammates lighting *every* empty space in
    the rift's row and column before the landing turn — which no group has
    ever used and which read as a stall.
    (c) During Niflheim's Embrace the engine checks after every turn that some
    gate is still reachable by all four souls with a completable rune set
    (counting only circles in that gate's road network, and crediting the
    rare survivable rift-crossing whose row and column are fully tiled or
    lit). The board only shrinks once the stack dies, so the first failure
    of this check is final.

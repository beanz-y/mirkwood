How to survive Myrkviðr

The goal

Win together: all four souls standing on the same Gate, each bearing a different rune of that gate's set. Lose together if both Gates are lost, the runes can no longer be gathered, a falling soul has nothing to land on — or the mist simply cuts you off.

Your turn

  • Move one space along a connected path. New paths are revealed around you (one drawn tile per open passage) — and everything your group's hope no longer lights is lost forever.
  • Stay instead: gain 1 Resolve ◆ (max 2), but standing still burns the top tile of the stack. If it's a Draugr, it claws onto a path beside you.
  • After moving you may spend Resolve to move again (up to twice).

Hope

  • Hopeful — you light all connected paths one space around you (no diagonals).
  • Hopeless — you see only your own tile. You must move every turn (staying costs 1 ◆), and you move blind: entering the mist reveals only the tile you step onto.
  • Rekindle — stand beside a hopeful soul on a connected path (automatic), or spend 1 ◆ at the start of your turn.

Resolve ◆ (max 2)

Gain it by Staying, or by evading a Draugr you triggered. Spend 1 to: Move again · Rekindle · Endure (stay while hopeless) · Brace (lose 2 tiles instead of 3 when struck) · Charge a Draugr (see below) · Sustain (skip Niflheim's toll).

The Draugr

They strike when anyone moves along, into, or out of a straight unbroken pathway connected to them — in all four directions, wrapping around the board's edges. Every soul in sight is struck: 3 tiles burned per victim, and their hope is extinguished. Walls, gaps, and Void Rifts block their sight. Ending your move out of the line evades the strike — and steels your Resolve.

Charging (1 ◆): step deliberately onto a Draugr's tile. Its strike always lands on you (and everything else it can see) — but once its attack is spent, the Draugr is banished from the forest, and you scramble onto an adjacent space. The only way to destroy one.

Fractured paths & Void Rifts

Cracked tiles crumble into Void Rifts when you leave them (or stay on them). Falling ends your turn; next turn you land on a drawn tile anywhere in the rift's row or column — hopeless. If the stack is empty when you must land, you fall forever.

Runes & Gates

Step into a Rune Circle to take one mark (it replaces your old one — marks can never be traded). Gates have one doorway, are permanent once placed, and hold any number of souls.

Niflheim's Embrace

When the last tile is drawn, no new paths can ever be revealed — and at the end of every turn the group must surrender one tile from the board (1 ◆ to Sustain). Reach a gate before the forest is gone.

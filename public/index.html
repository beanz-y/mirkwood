How to survive Myrkviðr

Welcome to Myrkviðr

No skald will sing of how you died. No shield-wall broke around you, no valkyrie stooped out of the storm to carry you up to the Allfather's benches. Yours was a straw-death — quiet, unwitnessed, unchosen — and what met you on the far side of it was not the mead-bright roar of a hall. It was wind moving through branches.

You stand beneath the black boughs of Myrkviðr, the dark wood that has marked the border between the worlds since the first days. Swan-maids have flown through it; gods and heroes have ridden its edges; the sons of Muspell will cross it burning when the world ends. Nothing in it keeps its shape. The mist of Niflheim breathes between the trunks, and wherever it settles, the forest forgets its own paths — a road walked once is not there when you turn around.

Yet the dead here whisper of two doors. Somewhere under the boughs stands Valgrind, the corpse-gate of Valhalla, behind which the einherjar feast and fight until Ragnarök calls them out. And somewhere stands the gate of Fólkvangr, Freyja's field, where her hall Sessrúmnir rises and she seats those dead she chooses as her own. Odin did not choose you; Freyja did not call your name. So you will make the gates take you: find the standing stones where the old rune-rings wait, cut their marks into your soul, and come before a gate that cannot refuse four souls bearing its four runes.

You carry no candle or torch — such fires died with your body. What you carry is an ember of hope, and it is enough: where your hope shines, the paths hold. Where it moves on, the mist devours what it leaves behind. And you are not alone beneath the boughs. The draugar walk here — the restless dead who sought the gates before you and failed, and rose again with nothing left in them but spite for every soul that still dares to hope.

Mirkwood (Myrkviðr) is a cooperative game of paths and dwindling light: four souls, played by one to four players, who escape together or not at all. Each turn your hope kindles fresh paths at the edge of the light, while the mist devours everything left unlit — the forest is never the same shape twice. The shared path stack is the party's hope made visible; every tile drawn, burned, or lost to the dark brings Niflheim's Embrace a step closer. Gather four different runes of one gate, bring all four souls to stand on it together, and walk out of Myrkviðr — or let the mist take everything.

The goal

Win together: all four souls standing on the same Gate, each bearing a different rune of that gate's set — Valgrind, the corpse-door of Odin's Valhalla, or the gate to Fólkvangr, Freyja's field, where her hall Sessrúmnir stands. Lose together if both Gates are lost, the runes can no longer be gathered (a circle a soul stands on is already spent — its stones speak only once), a soul falls with the stack spent — or, in Niflheim's Embrace, no road remains by which all four souls could reach a winnable gate. The forest knows when hope is gone, and says so at once.

Your turn

  • Move one space along a connected path. Your hope kindles the ways ahead — a fresh tile joins the board at every open passage of your new tile — and everything the group's hope no longer lights is lost forever.
  • Stay instead: gain 1 Resolve ◆ (max 2), but hope gutters while you linger — the top tile of the stack burns. If it's a Draugr, it claws onto a path beside you.
  • After moving you may spend Resolve to press on (up to two more steps).

Hope

  • Hopeful — you light all connected paths one space around you (no diagonals).
  • Hopeless — you see only your own tile. You must move every turn (staying costs 1 ◆), and you move blind: entering the mist reveals only the tile you step onto.
  • Rekindle — stand beside a hopeful soul on a connected path (automatic), or spend 1 ◆ at the start of your turn.

Resolve ◆ (max 2)

Gain it by Staying, or by evading a Draugr you roused. Spend 1 to: Press on (another step) · Rekindle · Endure (stay while hopeless) · Brace (lose 2 tiles instead of 3 when struck) · Berserk (rush a Draugr — see below) · Ward (skip Niflheim's toll).

The Draugar

The restless dead of Myrkviðr — they failed the road, and hate any light that still hopes. A Draugr wakes at motion in its corridors: step anywhere along a straight, unbroken run of path joined to it — however far, even across the board's wrapped edge — and it lashes down all four of its corridors at once. Every soul caught in its gaze is struck: 3 tiles burned per victim, and their hope is extinguished. Walls, gaps, and Void Rifts blind it. Ending your move outside its gaze means the strike misses you — every other soul still in its sight suffers it — and slipping its gaze steels your Resolve (+1).

Berserk (1 ◆): rush a Draugr's tile in a fury. Its strike always lands on you (and on everything else it can see) — but once its spite is spent, the Draugr is banished from the forest, and you claw your way onto an adjacent space. A Draugr can also be left behind: slip its gaze, or walk away — one that no soul lights any longer fades back into the mist.

Fractured paths & Void Rifts

Cracked tiles crumble into Void Rifts when you leave them (or stay on them). Falling ends your turn; next turn you land on a drawn tile anywhere in the rift's row or column — ember still lit, kindling the ways around you. If the stack is empty when you must land, you fall forever.

Runes & Gates

Step into a Rune Circle to take one mark (it replaces your old one — marks can never be traded). With the host's Random Runes option, the stones choose instead: you receive a random rune not currently borne by any soul — and you may linger: Staying on a circle doesn't crumble it, and the stones choose again each turn (a tile still burns from the stack; the circle crumbles when you leave). Gates have one doorway and hold any number of souls; once placed the everyday mist cannot take a Gate — only Niflheim's Embrace can (see below). A Gate still buried in the stack can burn away — watch the tile counts — and once a gate is wholly lost its runes are sealed: the stones no longer offer that pantheon's marks.

Niflheim's Embrace

When the last tile is drawn, no new paths can ever be kindled — and at the end of every turn the group must surrender one tile from the board (1 ◆ to Ward). The cold takes any tile still lit — even a Gate or a Void Rift — sparing only one a soul stands on. Reach a gate before the forest is gone.

NIFLHEIM’S EMBRACE

The path stack is spent — the last ember of shared hope goes dark, and the primordial cold closes in.

  • No new paths can ever be kindled.
  • At the end of every soul’s turn, the mist claims one tile from the board — unless that soul spends 1 ◆ to Ward the forest.

Reach a gate before the forest is gone.
